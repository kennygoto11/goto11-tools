🤖 Your First Day with Claude

A Setup Guide in 12 Panels — by someone who's been there
1
Meet Claude
👩‍💻  🤝  🧠
Think of Claude as a thinking partner, not a search engine. The more context you give, the better it gets — and it keeps learning about you over time.
Key mindset: You're building a working relationship, not typing queries.
2
Set Up Your Profile
⚙️ ✨
First stop: Settings. Tell Claude who you are and how you like to work.
  • Go to Settings → Profile
  • Add your name and what you do
  • Set User Preferences — tone, format, anything you always want
  • Choose a Style if you want Claude to write a certain way
Why it matters: These apply to every new conversation automatically!
3
Turn On Memory
🧠 💾
Claude can remember things between conversations — your projects, preferences, and context — so you don't repeat yourself every time.
  • Go to Settings → Memory
  • Toggle it ON
  • You can say "Remember that I…" to teach it things
  • You can say "Forget that…" to remove anything
4
Enable Your Features
🔍 📊 📁
Claude can do way more than chat. Turn on the good stuff!
  • Web Search — real-time info from the internet
  • Artifacts — code, apps, and interactive widgets
  • File Creation — Word docs, spreadsheets, PDFs
  • Research Mode — deep dives on complex topics
Check these in Settings → Features
5
🗂️  Create Projects — This Is the Big One
Projects are like dedicated workspaces. Each one keeps its own instructions, files, and context separate. Instead of one massive conversation about everything, you get focused environments for each area of your life or work.
✍️
Writing
Blogs, articles, posts, emails
💼
Work
Job tasks, meeting prep, strategy
🏠
Home & Family
Meals, kids, logistics
🌱
Personal
Health, learning, hobbies
The key move: Add Project Instructions to each one — who you are in that context, what you're working on, and how you want Claude to respond. Claude reads these every time you open a chat in that project. It's like onboarding a team member for each area of your life.
6
Connect Your Tools
🔌 🔗
Claude can plug into apps you already use — so it can read, search, and act inside them.
  • Google Calendar — check & create events
  • Gmail — search & draft emails
  • Notion — read & create pages
  • Slack — search & send messages
Find these in Settings → Integrations
7
Get the Desktop App
🖥️ 📂
The desktop app lets you connect Claude to folders on your computer — so it can read your actual files without uploading them every time.
  • Download from claude.ai/download
  • Go to Settings → Files
  • Add folders Claude can access (e.g. Documents, Desktop, a project folder)
  • Then just say "Look at the draft in my Documents folder"
Perfect for writers: Point it at your drafts folder and Claude can review, edit, and reference your files directly.
8
Talk to It Like a Person
💬 🎯
You don't need special commands. Just be specific about what you want.
❌ "Help me with dinner."
✅ "I've got chicken thighs, rice, and broccoli. Give me a quick 30-minute recipe for 2 people — nothing too spicy."
The formula: Context + what you need + any constraints = great output.
9
⛽  Don't Burn Through Your Fuel
Claude runs on tokens — think of them like fuel. Every message you send and every reply Claude gives costs tokens. Your plan has a daily/monthly limit, so being smart about usage means you'll never hit a wall mid-task.
🪫 Burns Tokens Fast
  • Pasting in an entire 30-page document when you only need page 5
  • Letting one conversation run for 50+ messages (context balloons)
  • Asking for a huge output when a short answer would do
  • Re-explaining your background in every single chat
  • Using the most powerful model for simple quick questions
🔋 Stays Efficient
  • Paste only the relevant section — not the whole thing
  • Start fresh conversations when switching topics
  • Tell Claude the length you want: "keep it to 3 bullets"
  • Use Projects & Memory so context is already loaded
  • Use Sonnet for quick tasks, Opus for complex ones
Think of it this way: A 100-message conversation doesn't use 100× the tokens — it uses way more, because Claude re-reads the entire conversation history with every reply. Shorter, focused chats are your best friend.
10
Name Your Chats
🏷️ ✏️
Claude auto-names chats, but they're often vague. Rename them yourself so you can actually find things later.
❌ "Untitled"
❌ "Help with email"
✅ "Q2 Board Deck — Draft 1"
✅ "Meal Prep — Week of Apr 7"
Tip: Click the chat title at the top to rename it. Future you will thank present you.
11
Build Custom Skills
🛠️ ⚡
If you do the same type of writing often, you can teach Claude your exact method by saving a skill file inside a Project.
  • A skill = instructions + rules + examples that Claude follows every time
  • Think: a recipe card for how you want a specific type of content made
Writer's Starter Skills:
📝 Blog Post Skill — your structure, tone, word count, CTA style
📧 Email Drafting Skill — your voice, sign-off, level of formality
📣 Social Post Skill — hook style, hashtag rules, platform format
📰 Article Skill — research depth, section flow, citation style
How: Upload a skill file (a text doc with your instructions) into your Project's knowledge base. Claude reads it automatically in every new chat.
12
🚀  The Secret: Build on Every Conversation
Claude gets better the more you use it. Every conversation teaches it about your preferences, your world, and how you think. That's the real advantage — cumulative context.
  • Don't start over every time — build on past chats
  • Tell Claude when it gets something right (or wrong)
  • Use "Remember that..." to lock in important things
  • Upload files, share links, paste in real content
  • Think of it as a co-worker who shows up ready every day
