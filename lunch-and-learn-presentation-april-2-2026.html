Step 2 of 5

Feed It to Claude

Here's roughly the prompt I used:

Here are my top 9 LinkedIn posts by engagement, and my 3 worst performers. Analyze what the top posts have in common that the underperformers don't. Look at: - Hook structure (what makes you stop scrolling?) - Post format (how does it unfold?) - Language patterns (sentence length, word choice, specificity) - Topic patterns (what themes drive engagement?) - CTA patterns (how do they close?) Be specific — show me the actual patterns, not generic advice. Also look at any visuals or images that accompanied each post — that's a critical engagement signal too.

The Cost Thread

90% of my top posts connected communication to a business outcome: revenue, career speed, decision velocity.

What Underperformers Did

Vague openings. Scattered ideas. Told instead of showed. Missing the cost layer entirely.

This is your version of "what works." Not what works on LinkedIn generally — what works when you post, for your ICP, for your offer. That's where the confidence comes from.

Step 4 of 5

Build Your Skill

Turn patterns into a reusable system — a Claude skill document:

Based on these patterns, build me a custom skill for writing LinkedIn posts. It should include: - Voice guardrails (what I sound like and what I don't) - Craft principles (sentence length, specificity, cost-threading) - A format library based on my top structures - A pre-publish checklist The skill should be specific to MY voice and MY audience — not generic.
